- *Supply and Demand Model*
  + *Demand -* How much people want it! /(The greater the demand, the higher the price)/
  + *Supply -* How much is available /(The greater the supply, the lower the price)/
  + *Equilibrium point -* The point in which both the consumer and the provider are happy to purchase and provides the goods /(This is where the "demand" curve intersects with the "supply" curve)/ \\

** DONE Utility Functions and Marginal Utility :content:

:ID: 495618d0-7311-43fd-b470-0eb48035afb3

- *Utility function -* A mathematical expression that translates bundles of goods into a single valuation of utility
- *Marginal Utility -* The change in utility from each additional good you consume

#+name: Dimishing Margin utility
#+begin_quote org
[!NOTE]
With each extra unit of a good increase =utility= by less than the previous unit of the good this is called *Diminishing Margin Utility* /(DMU)/
#+end_quote

#+name: Difference in DMU
#+begin_quote org
[!TIP]
Different goods come with different level of =DMU= /e.g. socks vs newspaper/
#+end_quote

#+name: DMU examples
#+begin_quote org
One of the best examples of diminishing marginal utility is how stores price different size of goods. It shows how =utility= and =supply-and-demand= works together
#+end_quote

- Demand is high from *some* drink, but NOT so high for *additional* drink
  + On a hot day demand for a drinks is higher but demand for *additional* drink will be lower -> /Diminishing marginal utility in action/
  + Lower price could be charge for *additional* drink

#+name: Keypoints

*** Introduction to Budget Constraints
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: aad77c92-d32d-4035-bfd5-76b714e51de6
:END:

- *Budget Constraint properties:*
  + Assuming individual *budget constraint* is their *incomes* /-> living from paycheck to paycheck/
  + Form a horizontal line graphically
  + *Opportunity cost* is represented by *price ratio* /(slope of the budget constraint curve)/ \\

#+name: Price Ratio and Slop
#+begin_quote org
[!NOTE]
Opportunity cost can be represented by the value of the =slope= of a curve that is: $$m = \frac{\Delta{y}}{\Delta{x}}$$ 
#+end_quote
*** Budget Constraints with Changing Income and Prices

#+name: Keypoints
#+begin_quote org
Is it possible to manipulate the budget constraints to represent a change in price or a change in income
#+end_quote

- *Change in income*
  + =slope= stays the same
  + new =budget curve= is parallel to original curve \\

- *Change in price*
  + =slope= is altered
  + new =budget curve= can shift inwards/outwards
