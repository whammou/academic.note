- *Terminologies:*
  + *Opportunity Cost* - The cost of any action in terms of what you could have done instead

  + *Constrained Optimization*
    * Constrained -> Every decision has an =opportunity cost=
    * Optimization -> Make yourself as well off as possible

  + *Model*

- *Supply and Demand Model*
  + *Demand -* How much people want it! /(The greater the demand, the higher the price)/
  + *Supply -* How much is available /(The greater the supply, the lower the price)/
  + *Equilibrium point -* The point in which both the consumer and the provider are happy to purchase and provides the goods /(This is where the "demand" curve intersects with the "supply" curve)/ \\

- *Command Economy:*
  + Trying to even things out among people /e.g. Soviet Union economy/
  + Government decides the economy /(Too much to handle -> shortages, corruption, waiting list, etc)/

- *Market Economy:*
  + Don't try to even things out instead let,
  + Individual decides their own needs and production

- *Preferences:*
  + Can be either =indifferent= or =prefer=
  + Logically consistent
  + More is better /(to some extend)/
  + =preference curve= - A condensed or simplify preference of some aspects

- *Properties of a map of indifference curve:*
  1. =consumer= prefer higher indifference curves
  2. Are downward sloping
  3. Never cross
  4. There is *one* indifference curve through each possible =bundle= \\
